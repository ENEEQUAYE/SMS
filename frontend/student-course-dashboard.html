Course Overview

Course Description

This comprehensive program prepares students to become Sterile Processing Technicians, responsible for ensuring the proper sterilization, packaging, and storage of medical equipment and supplies. Students will learn the principles and practices of sterile processing, including:

Course Objectives:

1. Understand the importance of sterile processing in healthcare

2. Learn sterilization methods, including autoclaving, dry heat, and ethylene oxide

3. Understand disinfection and cleaning procedures

4. Familiarize with processing and packaging of medical equipment and supplies

5. Learn quality control and quality assurance procedures

6. Understand regulations and standards governing sterile processing


Key Topics

1. Sterilization and Disinfection: Methods, principles, and applications

2. Processing and Packaging: Preparation, wrapping, and sterilization of medical equipment and supplies

3. Cleaning and Decontamination: Procedures for cleaning and decontaminating medical equipment and supplies

4. Quality Control and Assurance: Monitoring and maintaining quality in sterile processing

5. Regulations and Standards: Compliance with regulatory agencies and industry standards

6. Patient Safety and Infection Control: Principles and practices for ensuring patient safety and preventing infections

Certification:

Upon successful completion of the program, students will be eligible to take the Certified Registered Central Service Technician (CRCST) exam, offered by the Healthcare Sterile Processing Association (HSPA)
